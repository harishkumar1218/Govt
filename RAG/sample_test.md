

### Question 1
Which philosopher is known for their contributions to political thought that influenced both liberal and socialist ideologies?

Options:
A) Plato
B) Aristotle
C) John Stuart Mill
D) Friedrich Engels

Proposed Answer: C
Explanation: John Stuart Mill was a prominent figure in the history of political philosophy who made significant contributions to liberalism and utilitarianism. His ideas were influential in shaping modern democratic societies and his work has been foundational in discussions around individual rights and social welfare.

### Question 2
**Question Text:** In the context of Indian nationalism, how did Gandhi's approach differ from other nationalist leaders like Nehru and Patel?

**Options:**
A) Gandhi focused solely on non-violent resistance, while Nehru and Patel advocated for violent revolution.
B) Gandhi emphasized civil disobedience and satyagraha, whereas Nehru and Patel supported militant movements.
C) Gandhi was more radical than Nehru and Patel, advocating for immediate independence without parliamentary processes.
D) Gandhi and Nehru and Patel had identical strategies but differed in implementation.

**Answer:** B)
**Explanation:** Mahatma Gandhi's approach to Indian nationalism was characterized by non-violent resistance through methods such as civil disobedience and satyagraha. He believed in peaceful protests and dialogue with the British authorities rather than resorting to violence. This contrasts with the more militant approaches taken by Jawaharlal Nehru and Vallabhbhai Patel, who favored revolutionary tactics and sometimes engaged in armed struggle against colonial rule.

### Question 3
**Question Text:** How does the Indian Constitution reflect its historical legacies under British rule?

**Options:**
A) By incorporating elements of British legal systems into the constitution.
B) Through the direct influence of British monarchs on constitutional decisions.
C) By maintaining a completely separate legal system from British influences.
D) Allowing complete autonomy from any British legislative influence.

**Answer:** A)
**Explanation:** The Indian Constitution reflects its historical legacies under British rule primarily through its incorporation of various aspects of British legal systems and governance structures. It includes provisions for judicial review, fundamental rights, and directive principles derived from British constitutional law and practice. These elements serve to ensure continuity with the past while also providing a framework for modern governance.
