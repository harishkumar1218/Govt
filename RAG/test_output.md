

### Question 1
Which of the following statements best describes the primary contribution of the Chola Empire during the period of "Indian Feudalism"?

A) The establishment of a centralized state with a strong bureaucracy
B) The development of maritime trade networks across Southeast Asia
C) The promotion of Buddhism as the state religion
D) The expansion into Central Asia and Afghanistan

**Answer:** B
**Explanation:** During the Chola Empire's rule, there was indeed a shift towards more decentralized governance compared to earlier dynasties. However, they did not establish a centralized state with a strong bureaucracy. Their focus was more on expanding their empire through military campaigns rather than developing a bureaucratic system. They also promoted Hinduism as the state religion but did not expand into Central Asia and Afghanistan. Maritime trade networks were developed under their patronage, which is reflected in the extensive trade relations with Southeast Asian states.

### Question 2
Which of the following statements best describes the economic conditions of women in early medieval India?

Options:
A) Women had equal rights and opportunities as men.
B) Women were primarily responsible for agricultural work and household management.
C) Women enjoyed higher social status and educational opportunities.
D) Women were largely confined to domestic roles and faced limited economic independence.

Proposed Answer: D
Explanation: In early medieval India, particularly during the Chola and Pandya Empires, women were often confined to domestic roles due to societal norms that emphasized gender segregation. While they may have been involved in certain activities related to agriculture and household management, they generally lacked economic independence and were not typically engaged in formal employment outside the home. This reflects the broader patriarchal structure of these societies where women's participation in public life was restricted.
